Referral Guidelines

When to Refer for
Endodontic Treatment

A clinical decision guide for general practitioners.

Irreversible Pulpitis

Spontaneous, lingering, or severe tooth discomfort indicating irreversible pulp inflammation requiring root canal treatment.

Pulp Necrosis with Periapical Pathology

Non-vital tooth with radiographic evidence of periapical radiolucency requiring endodontic intervention.

Retreatment of Failed Root Canal

Previously treated tooth with persistent symptoms, recurrent infection, or radiographic evidence of treatment failure.

Calcified Canals

Teeth with obliterated or severely calcified canals requiring microscopic magnification and specialised techniques for negotiation.

Internal or External Resorption

Resorptive defects requiring specialist assessment for treatment viability and approach planning.

Cracked Tooth Syndrome

Suspected or confirmed incomplete fractures requiring specialist evaluation with transillumination and microscopic examination.

Dental Trauma

Luxation injuries, avulsion, root fractures, or other traumatic injuries requiring specialist assessment and management.

Complex Anatomy

C-shaped canals, dens invaginatus, extra roots, or other anatomical variations requiring advanced instrumentation and imaging.

Apicoectomy / Endodontic Surgery

Cases where non-surgical retreatment is not feasible or has failed, requiring microsurgical intervention.

Medically Complex Patients

Patients with significant medical histories (anticoagulation, bisphosphonate therapy, immunosuppression) requiring specialist management.
