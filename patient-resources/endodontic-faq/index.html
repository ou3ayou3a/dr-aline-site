Common Questions

Endodontic FAQ

General

What is a root canal treatment?
Root canal treatment is a procedure to treat infection or inflammation inside the tooth. The specialist removes the affected pulp tissue, cleans and shapes the canal system, and seals the tooth to prevent future infection. The goal is to save the natural tooth and restore it to full function.
What is endodontics?
Endodontics is the branch of dentistry that deals with the diagnosis and treatment of conditions affecting the dental pulp (the tissue inside the tooth) and the surrounding tissues. Endodontists are specialist dentists who focus exclusively on this area.
Why should I see a specialist endodontist?
Specialist endodontists complete additional years of advanced training focused exclusively on root canal treatment and related procedures. They use operating microscopes and advanced imaging technology (like CBCT scans) for more precise diagnosis and treatment — particularly valuable for complex cases, retreatments, and difficult anatomy.

The Procedure

What happens during a root canal?
After thorough numbing, the endodontist creates a small opening in the tooth to access the canals. Using the operating microscope for magnification, the infected or inflamed pulp tissue is carefully removed. The canals are cleaned, shaped, and disinfected, then filled with a biocompatible material and sealed. Your general dentist will then place a final restoration (usually a crown) to protect the tooth.
How long does a root canal take?
Most root canal treatments can be completed in one or two appointments. The duration depends on the complexity of the case, the number of canals, and whether it is a primary treatment or a retreatment. Dr. Aline will provide a personalised time estimate during your consultation.
Is endodontic treatment uncomfortable?
Modern endodontic treatment is performed under thorough local anaesthesia. Most patients report that the experience is comparable to having a filling placed. Dr. Aline takes particular care to ensure patient comfort throughout the procedure and explains each step as it happens.

After Treatment

What can I expect after treatment?
Some sensitivity in the treated area is normal for a few days following the procedure. This can typically be managed with over-the-counter medication as recommended by Dr. Aline. You will receive detailed aftercare instructions specific to your case. It is important to follow up with your general dentist for the permanent restoration.
Will I need a crown after a root canal?
In most cases, yes. A root canal-treated tooth benefits from a crown or similar restoration to provide structural protection. Dr. Aline will provide a recommendation and communicate directly with your general dentist regarding the optimal restorative approach and timing.
Can a root canal treatment be repeated?
Yes. If a previously treated tooth develops new problems or if the initial treatment was incomplete, endodontic retreatment may be recommended. This involves removing the existing filling material, re-cleaning the canals, and re-sealing the tooth. Dr. Aline specialises in retreatment cases.

Booking

How do I book an appointment?
You can book through our contact page, by phone, or via WhatsApp. If you have been referred by your dentist, they may submit a referral through our online referral portal and we will contact you directly to schedule.
Does insurance cover endodontic treatment?
Most dental insurance plans provide coverage for endodontic treatment. We recommend contacting your insurance provider to confirm your coverage details. Our team can assist with pre-authorisation and provide treatment cost estimates during your consultation.
